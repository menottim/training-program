🎯 Strength Targets

Your fastest path to jumping higher is getting stronger. Plyometrics on a weak base produce minimal returns.

The Key Metric for Vertical Jump Research shows a trap bar deadlift of 2.0–2.5x bodyweight is the benchmark for elite jumping. At your current strength levels, every pound added to the bar translates directly to inches on your vertical.

🧰 Achilles Tendinopathy Protocol

Bilateral management. Heavy Slow Resistance (HSR) produces outcomes equivalent to eccentric-only loading with higher compliance (Beyer et al. 2015, Am J Sports Med). 3×/week, integrated into training days.

Protocol (3x/week: Wed, Fri, + 1 Recovery Day)

3 sets per session, 6-second tempo (3s concentric / 3s eccentric). Include both seated (bent-knee, soleus bias) and standing (straight-knee, gastroc bias) variants. Optional: single-leg heel drop 3×15 on a lighter day for unilateral work.

Progressive 12-week load (Beyer protocol)

WeeksLoadReps per set
115RM15
2-312RM12
4-510RM10
6-88RM8
9-126RM6

Heavy must be heavy. If every rep feels easy, add weight — slow tempo alone does not guarantee adequate strain. Working weight should stay above 70% 1RM across the set (Morrison & Cook 2022).

Pain-monitoring rules (Silbernagel framework)

  • 0-3/10 during loading: acceptable, continue protocol. Pain at this level is expected during loaded tendon work and does not indicate harm.
  • 4-5/10 during loading: maintain current load, do not progress. If persists 2+ weeks, reduce load 15-20%.
  • >5/10 during loading: stop the exercise. Reduce load 25% next session. If >5 persists after reduction, flag for clinical review.
  • 24-hour rule: pain should return to pre-exercise baseline within 24 hours. If still elevated the next day, load was too high — reduce 10-15% next session.
  • Morning stiffness >30 min (up from typical <15 min): regression signal. Reduce sport minutes or reactive deload.
  • Game-day achilles pain >5/10: drop Friday plyos for 2 weeks, temporarily increase HSR to 4x/week.
  • Always step down from box jumps — never rebound.
  • Maintain HSR at full load during lifting deloads — tendons have months-long adaptation cycles (Magnusson & Kjaer 2019).

Adjuncts worth trialing (Emerging evidence)

  • Collagen peptides or gelatin + vitamin C: 10-15g collagen/gelatin + ~50mg vit C, 30-60 min before loading. Mechanistic support (Shaw 2017); pilot clinical data in Achilles (Praet 2019).
  • ESWT if HSR plateaus at 12 weeks for midportion (not insertional) — combined with continued loading (Paantjens 2022).

Not recommended

  • PRP injection. No benefit over saline when added to loading (de Vos et al. 2010, JAMA).
  • GTN patches. Weak/mixed evidence, meaningful headache side effect.

Protein Distribution Target ~0.4 g/kg per meal across 4 meals (~40g each). Schoenfeld & Aragon 2018 shows distribution beats total-alone when the daily total is already in range. Morton 2018 meta-analysis places the muscle-building ceiling at 1.62 g/kg/day with the upper-end tail extending to ~2.2 g/kg.
Carb Timing Higher carbs on game days (Sun, Tue) and training days (Wed, Thu, Fri). Lower carbs on recovery days (Mon, Sat). This fuels performance without excess.
The "anabolic window" is 4–6 hours, not 30 minutes Aragon & Schoenfeld 2013 (J Int Soc Sports Nutr) showed the post-workout protein window is wider than gym culture suggests. Total daily protein and distribution matter far more than narrow post-session timing. A protein-containing meal within a few hours post-training is fully sufficient.
Alcohol cost Parr 2014 (PLoS ONE) showed post-exercise alcohol suppresses myofibrillar protein synthesis ~24% even with co-ingested protein. Dose is monotonic, and even 3–4 drinks disrupt REM sleep in the second half of the night. Cap at 1–2 drinks on training days, not within 3 hours of bed.
Why Recomp Matters for Your Vertical Every 1 lb of fat lost is dead weight you no longer lift off the ground. A 10 lb fat loss (while maintaining muscle) makes you ~5% more powerful relative to bodyweight — measurable inches on your vertical with zero strength gains needed.

🔄 Deload Protocol

Reactive default (not strict calendar)

Scheduled every 4th week as a safety net (Weeks 4, 8, 12...), but skip the scheduled deload when fatigue signals are absent. Coleman et al. 2024 (PeerJ) RCT showed prophylactic 1-week deloads in non-fatigued lifters reduced strength gains without hypertrophy benefit. Use the triggers below instead.

How to deload (when triggered)

  • Cut volume 40-50% (sets in half: 4×5 to 2×5, 3×8 to 2×8)
  • Maintain intensity at 85-95% of working weights. Dropping load too far causes detraining without added recovery benefit (Pritchard et al. 2015)
  • Achilles HSR stays at full load — tendons have months-long adaptation cycles (Magnusson & Kjaer 2019)
  • Prioritize sleep, nutrition, soft-tissue work
  • Play games normally — deload applies to lifting, not sport

Triggers (any 2+ warrant a 4-7 day cut)

  • RPE 1-2 points higher than prior week at same weight
  • Performance drops >5% at matched RPE
  • Achilles morning stiffness >30 min (up from <15 min typical)
  • Sleep quality declining for 3+ consecutive nights
  • Resting HR elevated >5 bpm for 3+ days
  • Persistent mood disturbance or loss of motivation (Meeusen 2013 overtraining criteria)

⚠️ When to Modify

Achilles pain > 5/10 during games
Drop Friday plyos for 2 weeks, temporarily increase HSR to 4x/week
Poor sleep (< 6 hrs) for 3+ days
Keep volume, drop intensity 10-15% on compounds. Knowles 2018: sleep debt hits squat/bench/deadlift more than isolation work, so swap to single-joint work if needed.
Strength plateau 3+ weeks at same weight
1) Check recovery (sleep, nutrition, game load). 2) Try microloading (2.5 lb / fractional plates). 3) Add one set at current weight (volume before intensity). Only then consider rep-scheme swap or 10% reset.
Knee or hip pain emerging
Add hip mobility work, reduce squat depth temporarily
Feeling great and fully recovered
Don't add volume — add load to existing sets
Golden Rule for Masters Athletes Recovery capacity is your limiting factor, not training capacity. Melatonin drops and cortisol rises with age. When in doubt, do less and sleep more. You can always add — you can't un-injure.
